Cultural Etiquette for Mexico & Spain

Respect local customs to enhance your experience and avoid faux pas. Below are key tips for both countries, expanded for comprehensiveness.

Mexico Etiquette Tips

  • Greet everyone with "Hola" or "Buenos días," even strangers—handshakes with eye contact are common. For close friends, a hug or cheek kiss.
  • Be polite and patient; use "por favor" and "gracias" liberally. Tip 10-15% for services like restaurants, taxis (round up), hotels (bellhops 20-50 pesos).
  • Say "Salud" if someone sneezes; use "Con permiso" to interrupt or pass by, "Provecho" when people are eating.
  • Life is relaxed—punctuality is flexible for social events (arrive 30-120 mins late); be on time for business/tours.
  • Dress modestly in religious sites (cover shoulders/knees); avoid topless or shorts in churches. Casual elsewhere, but smart for evenings.
  • Avoid direct "no"—be indirect to maintain harmony (e.g., "I'll think about it").
  • Embrace "mañana" mentality—things happen at a slower pace; don't rush.
  • Respect indigenous cultures; ask before photographing people or sacred sites.
  • In markets, bargaining is expected—start at half price, be friendly.
  • Avoid discussing sensitive topics like drug violence or politics unless initiated.
  • Family is central—greet all family members; bring small gifts if invited home.
  • Public displays of affection are common but modest.

Spain Etiquette Tips

  • Greet when entering shops/elevators; say hello/goodbye to everyone. Two cheek kisses for friends (starting right).
  • Respect regional differences (e.g., Catalonia speaks Catalan; avoid assuming all is "Spanish"—use "España" carefully).
  • Meals are late—lunch 2-4 PM, dinner 9-11 PM; linger after meals (sobremesa). Tapas are social—share.
  • Tipping is minimal—round up or leave small change (1-2 euros); no tip for taxis usually.
  • Be expressive; conversations are loud and animated—don't take it as rude.
  • Dress modestly in churches/cathedrals (high necklines, no shorts). Smart casual for cities; beachwear at coasts.
  • Siesta time (2-5 PM) means quiet in residential areas; shops may close in smaller towns.
  • Use formal "usted" initially; switch to "tú" when invited. "Vosotros" for groups.
  • Bargaining rare except markets; be polite in queues.
  • Avoid bullfighting discussions if controversial; respect fiestas (e.g., Running of Bulls).
  • Smoking common outdoors; ask before lighting up.
  • Public affection accepted; family-oriented—kids stay up late.

General for Both: Personal space closer than UK/US; eye contact shows respect. Learn local holidays (e.g., Día de Muertos in Mexico, La Tomatina in Spain). Sustainability: Use reusable items, respect nature.
